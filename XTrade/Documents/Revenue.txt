Definition:
Revenue is reported net of sales before deducting the cost of goods sold and operating expenses.

Why It Matter?
Financial Performance: 
Revenue is a primary measure of a company's financial performance. Increasing revenue indicates growth and success in generating demand for products or services.

Profitability: 
While revenue is essential, it does not necessarily equate to profitability. Companies need to manage costs effectively to ensure that revenue translates into profits.

Investor Confidence: 
Revenue growth is often a key metric that investors and stakeholders look for when assessing the health and potential of a company. Consistent revenue growth can instill confidence in investors.

Market Positioning: 
Revenue figures can reflect a company's market position relative to competitors. Higher revenue may indicate market dominance or successful differentiation strategies.
